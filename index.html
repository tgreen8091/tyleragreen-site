Stadium Fanatic

Date: July 2010

I developed a basic social network around stadium travel in an effort to learn PHP and MySQL. As you will notice, I had not yet learned how to make a website look decent in the slightest.

Stadium Fanatic was my first foray into web programming and was critical to my understanding of the modern web. Even better, it sparked my passion for stadium travel. I would not be well on my way to visiting all 30 MLB ballparks without this formative project!

Feel free to browse and even create a free account here: http://www.stadiumfanatic.com/.

Baseball Transit Authority Map

Date: November 2015

I like baseball. I like stadiums. I like transit. What better way to combine these interests than my learning the new skill of vector graphics? I have followed the Transit Maps blog casually for a few years, where Cam Booth uses the industry standard Adobe Illustrator to create transit maps. He recommends Inkscape is a reasonable alternative, and, best of all, it's free to download!

You will notice there are only 25 stations for the 30 MLB teams. This is because I made the design decision to combine multiple teams in Chicago, LA, New York, DC/Baltimore, and San Francisco, into single transit stops. Keep in mind, the map is entirely fictional, so this was purely a fun activity to familiarize myself with vector graphics. I now know about shapes, grids, paths, and nodes!

Click here to download the map as a PDF. And, as always, let me know about any recommendations you may have!
